We can do more than we can manage
Science and engineering gave us enormous power—AI, fossil fuels, biotech, global finance. Governance, ethics, and cooperation didn’t keep pace. Power without coordination is noisy at best, destructive at worst.

Climate pressure is the physical limit
Climate change isn’t just an environmental issue; it’s a systems problem. It stresses food supply, water, energy grids, migration patterns, and national security—all at once.

Inequality breaks feedback loops
Wealth and power concentration means decisions are made far from consequences. When feedback is delayed or ignored, bad designs persist—classic engineering failure.

Information chaos
We live in an era where information spreads faster than verification. Misinformation corrodes trust in institutions, experts, and even basic facts. No trust → no coordination.

Short-term incentives dominate
Political cycles, profit targets, and social media rewards favor fast gains over long-term stability. Think of it as optimizing for quarterly output while ignoring fatigue failure.
